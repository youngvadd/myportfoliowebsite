SQL Project

This project looks at product performance during 2022 and 2024 period from PixelPulse store dataset.

Here are the questions I was interested in answering:

1) How does our pricing compare to competitors across different stores and time periods?

2) Are there seasonal or cyclical patterns in pricing behavior?

3) Which store maintains the strongest competitive pricing advantage?

4) Do pricing changes correlate with shifts in sales volume or customer behavior?

I took the following steps to create my analysis:

Download the SQL document

1) Pulling data from Gumroad.

2) Identifying any duplicates, missing values or inconsistent date format.

3) Filtering tables to aggrigate KPIs.

4) Making a deep dive analysis in PowerBi.

Analysis:

1) The Total Sales chart from 2022 to 2024 reveals cyclical performance with peaks in August 2022, and a dip in early 2023. Sales show recovery post- March 2023, suggesting stabilization (Finance team).

2) The horizontal bar chart compares demand and product sold across 5 categories, showing perfect alignment in all cases. Groceries lead with 1.52M units, followed by Clothing .(Product team).

3) The lowest-performing category in was electronics, with only 0.16M units sold and demanded. This suggests minimal market interest or possible oversupply. Recommend a targeted review of the lowest performing category to uncover pricing, product fit, or visibility issues, and test strategic adjustments to improve traction.(Product team).

4) Groceries dominate sales across all seasons and regions, peaking in Summer. Clothing and Toys consistently underperform, with Winter showing the lowest figures. Regional differences are subtle, though the South leads slightly. (Marketing team).

5) Across all four stores, our pricing closely tracks competitor rates, with occasional undercuts and alignment. A notable dip in January 2023 suggests strategic discounting. Store S003 maintains the most competitive edge, while S001 shows minimal price differentiation. (Product team).
